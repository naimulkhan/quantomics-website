## About the Workshop

The **QuantOmics @ IEEE BIBM Workshop** is a half-day satellite event co-located with the IEEE International Conference on Bioinformatics and Biomedicine (BIBM). This is the second QuantOmics external event (Year 2 of the program; following the Year 1 launch), providing a premier international venue for trainees to present peer-reviewed research to global experts.

Additional QuantOmics workshops will be co-located with major IEEE conferences in Years 4 and 5, interspersed with the national symposiums in Years 3 and 6.

---

## Workshop Format

- **Invited talk** — Program Director or senior co-applicant presenting QuantOmics research vision
- **Trainee oral presentations** — 3–4 selected trainees presenting peer-reviewed work (15 minutes + Q&A)
- **Poster flash talks** — 2-minute spotlights from trainee poster presenters
- **Panel discussion** — "Bridging quantum sensing and biomedical AI: challenges and opportunities"

---

## Submission Guidelines

QuantOmics trainees who wish to present at this workshop should:

1. Submit an abstract (300–500 words) to the program coordinator by the deadline communicated via the trainee portal
2. Submissions are reviewed by the Steering Committee for quality and scientific fit
3. Selected presenters receive travel support from the QuantOmics program supplement

---

## Why Present at IEEE Conferences?

Presenting at a top-tier international IEEE conference provides QuantOmics trainees with:
- **Global visibility** — exposure to an international audience of experts in bioinformatics, biomedical engineering, and AI
- **Peer review experience** — submissions go through the conference review process
- **Networking** — building connections with future collaborators, postdoctoral supervisors, and industry contacts
- **CV development** — IEEE publications are highly valued in academic and industry career paths
