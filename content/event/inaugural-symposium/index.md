## About the Symposium

The **1st QuantOmics National Symposium** is a three-day event bringing together all QuantOmics stakeholders — trainees, faculty co-applicants, industry partners, government representatives, and invited international keynote speakers — to celebrate program progress, showcase trainee research, and forge new connections.

The symposium is held at **Toronto Metropolitan University** (Year 3 of the program). The 2nd Symposium will be held at **Université Laval** (Québec City) in Year 6.

---

## Program Highlights

### Day 1 — Research Showcase
- Opening keynote by Program Director Dr. Naimul Khan
- Invited keynote from an international quantum biosensing researcher
- Oral presentations by PhD trainees (20 minutes each)
- Poster session: MSc and undergraduate trainee posters
- Welcome reception

### Day 2 — Interdisciplinary Science & Translation
- Invited keynote: AI-driven precision medicine in clinical practice
- Panel: "From Quantum Sensor to the Clinic" — industry and clinical perspectives
- Oral presentations: cross-stream research highlights
- Innovation Studio showcase: team hackathon results
- Networking dinner

### Day 3 — Career Development & Job Fair
- **Concurrent Job Fair** — employer booths from industry, government, and academic partners including Epiloid Biotech, Ontario Genomics, Klick Health, Alimentiv, Novavax, and C2MI
- Career panel: "What employers really want from quantum-biomedical researchers"
- One-on-one networking sessions with recruiter representatives
- Program Committee town hall — open discussion with trainees
- Closing remarks and cohort recognition

---

## Who Should Attend?

All QuantOmics trainees (undergraduate, MSc, PhD, PDF) are expected to attend and present their research. Attendance by at least one QuantOmics supervisor from each institution is required.

External attendees — including researchers from partner and non-partner institutions, industry professionals, and government stakeholders — are welcome to register.

## Registration & Submission

Registration and abstract submission details will be circulated to all QuantOmics trainees and supervisors via the program coordinator.
