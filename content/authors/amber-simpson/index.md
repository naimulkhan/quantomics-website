Amber Simpson is Associate Professor in the School of Computing at Queen's University, where she holds a **Tier 2 Canada Research Chair in Biomedical Computing and Informatics**. Her research focuses on developing computational models to interpret complex biomedical data, with applications in cancer research and personalized medicine. She has trained **over 50 HQP** whose careers span academia, industry, and clinical research.

Dr. Simpson is also a Co-applicant on the active CREATE Medical Informatics program (Queen's), which focuses on AI ethics for electronic-health-record analytics — complementary to, but distinct from, QuantOmics' quantum sensing and genomics focus.

In QuantOmics, Dr. Simpson serves as **Institutional Lead at Queen's University**, co-leads **Stream 3 (AI-Powered Therapeutic Design)**, mentors trainees in applying AI to multimodal biomedical data, and co-teaches **Course 1.3 — AI in Genomics**.
